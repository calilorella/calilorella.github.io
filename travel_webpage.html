Travel to Paris!

From iconic bucket list attractions to picture perfect pleasures such as a picnic by the Seine River or a class where you'll learn how to make croissants,Paris offers something for everyone.

Here are 5 reasons why your next trip should be to Paris :

  1. Eiffel Tower

    2. The Eiffel Tower was built as the main entrance and a symbol
    of France's industrial and technological prowess for the
    1889 World's Fair in Paris. The tower now offers a series of
    fun events to help spruce up your trip to Paris such as
    many observation decks with a panoramic view of the city,
    multiple restaurants and cafes from fine dining to some
    more casual options, souvenir shops, historical displays,
    and lots more!

  2. La Seine

    3. La Seine, or the Seine River, is a gorgeous river in northern
    France that flows throughParis and goes into the English Channel at
    Le Havre. You can also take nice river cruises on la seine which are
    enjoyable because they offer a relaxing, and cool perspective of Paris
    and its iconic landmarks. This is especially very beautiful at night
    when all of Paris is illuminated. La seine is also open for public
    swimming in three designated water quality controlled areas in Paris
    as of July 2025! So whether you’re relaxing on a nice boat ride or
    even just walking along the streets beside the seine river, it is a
    very enjoyable experience.

  3. Louvre Museum

    4. The Louvre in Paris is the world's largest and most visited art
    museum with a large collection of art and different artifacts some of which are
    very old and rare including famous pieces like Leonardo da
    Vinci's Mona Lisa, the Venusde Milo, and the Winged Victory
    of Samothrace. Another thing the Louvre is known for is its
    iconic glass pyramid entrance which in itself is very cool
    to see! As France's national museum, the Louvre is a very
    big cultural landmark of Paris , attracting millions of
    visitors each year. It really is an amazing place to visit.

  4. Rue Cler

    5. Rue Cler is a famous and charming market street with many pedestrians
    the 7th arrondissement of Paris , so near many other popular site
    seeing activities such as the Louvre and the Eiffel Tower. Rule cle
    is known for its local atmosphere, many food shops, cafes, and
    proximity to the Eiffel Tower. It's a popular spot for local Parisians
    to buy groceries and for visitors to experience a traditional Parisian
    market street. There are many cute small stores on the market street
    where you could buy anything from flowers, to wine and cheese. So, if
    you’re looking to feel like a local, this is a lively small town
    atmosphere where local Parisians shop daily and dine at tables
    spilling onto the street,offering a classic insight into Parisian life.
    Some shops on Rue Cler include:

    • Mariage Frères
    • François Pralus
    • Roger Billebault

  5. Champs-Élysées

    6. The Champs-Élysées is a very well known long
    avenue in Paris . The Champs-Élysées is the perfect
    place if you’re interested in luxury shopping and
    visiting older art and architecture. You can walk
    up the Arc de Triomphe for very nice views of the
    city, visit the Grand Palais and Petit Palais for
    art and architecture, shop in the many luxury shops,
    watch a show at the Théâtre des Champs-Élysées, or
    even just enjoy a long walk along the avenue or
    relaxing in the Jardin des Champs-Élysées. It
    is definitely a must see if you’re visiting Paris

    Paris's Climate

    1. What is the Climate Zone in Paris?

      2. The climate zone in Paris is temperate and has mild and wet conditions throughout the year. This means that Paris has hot/warm summers and cool/cold winters with low temperatures, usually above freezing. Paris having a temperate climate also means that Paris has 4 distinct seasons including winter, spring, summer, and fall.

    3. Why Does Paris See This Temperature and Precipitation?

      4. Paris experiences a temperate maritime climate due to many factors including its latitude, ocean currents, and air currents.
      Factor 1:
      Paris does have seasonal changes due to having a latitude of 48.8567°N, with cool/cold winters and warm summers with average highs anywhere from 7°C in January to 25°C in July and August.
      Factor 2:
      The ocean current North Atlantic Drift, flows out of the Gulf Stream, and plays a big role in warming the air masses in Western Europe, which makes the winters in Paris less intense than in other areas of the same latitude.
      Factor 3:
      The westerly winds also cause moisture in the air that is carried by the Atlantic Ocean which then causes regular rainfall throughout the year (around 50-77 mm per month) and not much snowfall.

      These are the factors that explain why the temperature and precipitation data in my table is seen in Paris.

    4. Typical Vegetation and Fauna Found in Paris

      5. The typical vegetation of Paris is a mix of a mix of planted ornamental trees which include plane trees, horse chestnuts, lindens and maples, and more. Within more managed gardens like the Jardin des Plantes, thousands of plant species are planted, whereas less managed spaces such as sidewalk edges or other random spots have vegetation that include grasses and weeds and native plants like most cities would normally have. Examples of these wildlife habitats include green spaces such as the Bois de Boulogne and Bois de Vincennes. Some of the most common fauna include birds (pigeons, ducks), smaller mammals, like red foxes, hedgehogs, and a few different bat species. The city has ponds and canals where aquatic life can also be found. In general, Paris contains almost 2,800-2,900 species of wild organisms, which is quite a surprising biodiversity considering Paris is an urban ecosystem.

    5. Climate Change Challenges Paris is Facing

      6. There are quite a few big climate change challenges Paris is experiencing. These challenges include an increase in heatwaves and floods, water shortage, and infrastructure and public health stress. Increasing global temperatures and the urban heat island effect are making summers even hotter which can be dangerous to the health of Parisians and is also consuming more energy. The heavy rainfall has been increasing and becoming more intense causing areas that are generally lower to the ground or just may be lower than other parts of Paris, to flood and that is causing damage to infrastructure. Meanwhile, Paris is also having longer dry periods and an increase in the evaporation which is threatening water availability and puts trees, green areas and garden spaces at risk. Without enough water, this wildlife will struggle to survive. All of these challenges are all mainly caused by global warming, changing weather patterns, and having many people as well as buildings jammed into small areas. There are many vulnerable people in these areas who have higher health risks, have poor income or living conditions and this means they might suffer more than others.
